CLINICAL REFERENCE: Drug Interactions and Contraindications

ANTIBIOTIC DRUG INTERACTIONS
==============================

MACROLIDES (Azithromycin, Clarithromycin, Erythromycin)
--------------------------------------------------------
Azithromycin (Z-Pack):
- Warfarin interaction: MODERATE — azithromycin inhibits gut flora, increases warfarin effect
  Monitor INR closely; INR may rise by 20-50% within 3-5 days
- QT-prolonging drugs: SEVERE — additive QT prolongation, risk of torsades de pointes
  Avoid with: amiodarone, haloperidol, methadone, fluoroquinolones (if possible)
- Metformin: MINOR — no significant interaction
- Mechanism: weak CYP3A4 inhibitor (azithromycin less than clarithromycin)

Clarithromycin:
- Warfarin: MAJOR — significant CYP3A4 inhibition, can double INR
- Statins (atorvastatin, simvastatin): MAJOR — rhabdomyolysis risk
- Do not use with simvastatin/lovastatin

FLUOROQUINOLONES (Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin, Ciprofloxacin)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Levofloxacin / Moxifloxacin:
- Warfarin: MODERATE-MAJOR — increases anticoagulant effect, monitor INR
- QT prolongation: SIGNIFICANT — avoid in patients with baseline QT prolongation
  Additive with: amiodarone, antipsychotics, antifungals
- Antacids/iron/zinc: Reduce absorption by up to 80% — separate by 2 hours
- NSAIDs: Increased risk of seizures

BETA-LACTAMS (Penicillins, Cephalosporins)
-------------------------------------------
Amoxicillin / Amoxicillin-clavulanate:
- Warfarin: MODERATE — alters gut flora, can increase INR
- Metformin: MINOR — no significant interaction
- Oral contraceptives: MINOR — theoretical decrease in efficacy

ALLERGY CROSS-REACTIVITY
=========================

PENICILLIN ALLERGY:
- Cross-reactivity with Amoxicillin: HIGH (~80-90%) — AVOID
- Cross-reactivity with Ampicillin: HIGH (~80-90%) — AVOID
- Cross-reactivity with Piperacillin: HIGH — AVOID
- Cross-reactivity with Cephalosporins: LOW (~1-2%) — generally safe in non-anaphylactic allergy
  Exception: Cefazolin (first-gen) — higher cross-reactivity, AVOID in severe penicillin allergy
- Cross-reactivity with Carbapenems: VERY LOW (~0.5%) — generally safe
- Cross-reactivity with Aztreonam: MINIMAL — safe to use
- Azithromycin: NO cross-reactivity — SAFE in penicillin allergy
- Fluoroquinolones: NO cross-reactivity — SAFE in penicillin allergy

SULFA ALLERGY (Sulfonamides):
- Cross-reactivity with TMP-SMX (Bactrim): HIGH — AVOID
- Cross-reactivity with Furosemide: LOW (contains sulfonamide group, different structure)
  Generally safe, use caution in severe sulfa allergy
- Cross-reactivity with Thiazide diuretics: LOW — generally safe

NSAID ALLERGY:
- Cross-reactivity within NSAIDs: MODERATE — aspirin allergy may cross-react with ibuprofen
- COX-2 inhibitors (celecoxib): generally safe in aspirin allergy

CONTRAINDICATIONS BY CONDITION
================================

CHRONIC KIDNEY DISEASE (CKD) — ICD-10: N18.x
- Metformin: CONTRAINDICATED in CKD stage 4-5 (GFR < 30) — lactic acidosis risk
- NSAIDs: CONTRAINDICATED — worsen renal function, sodium retention
- Aminoglycosides: AVOID — nephrotoxic, accumulate in CKD
- Adjust: fluoroquinolones, beta-lactams, most antibiotics — dose adjustment required
  Levofloxacin: reduce dose in CKD (GFR < 50)

WARFARIN THERAPY — ICD-10: Z79.01
- Check ALL new medications for CYP2C9 interactions
- Major enhancers (increase INR): amiodarone, fluconazole, metronidazole, TMP-SMX, 
  clarithromycin, most antibiotics (gut flora alteration)
- Major reducers (decrease INR): rifampin, carbamazepine, barbiturates
- Monitor INR within 3-5 days of any new antibiotic

ATRIAL FIBRILLATION — ICD-10: I48.x
- QT-prolonging drugs: CAUTION — already at risk for arrhythmia
- Avoid: macrolides (if alternatives exist), fluoroquinolones with QT history

TYPE 2 DIABETES — ICD-10: E11.x
- Fluoroquinolones: CAUTION — can cause hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia, especially in patients on oral hypoglycemics
- Monitor blood glucose closely if fluoroquinolone prescribed
- Metformin + contrast dye: hold 48 hours before/after IV contrast

LIVER DISEASE — ICD-10: K70-K77
- Acetaminophen: REDUCE DOSE or AVOID in severe liver disease
- Statins: use caution in active liver disease
- Azithromycin: use caution in severe hepatic impairment

MYASTHENIA GRAVIS — ICD-10: G70.x
- Fluoroquinolones: CONTRAINDICATED — can exacerbate myasthenic symptoms
- Aminoglycosides: CONTRAINDICATED — neuromuscular blockade

SAFE ALTERNATIVES FOR COMMON CLINICAL SCENARIOS
=================================================

Scenario: CAP in patient with Penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis)
- Recommended: Azithromycin 500mg PO/IV daily (no cross-reactivity)
- Alternative: Levofloxacin 750mg PO/IV daily (no cross-reactivity)
- Avoid: Amoxicillin, Ampicillin, Amoxicillin-clavulanate, Piperacillin-tazobactam

Scenario: CAP in patient on Warfarin
- Recommended: Azithromycin (moderate interaction — monitor INR in 3-5 days)
- Alternative: Ceftriaxone (lower warfarin interaction than macrolides)
- Requires: INR check within 48-72 hours of starting antibiotic

Scenario: CAP in patient with CKD (Creatinine > 2.0)
- Recommended: Azithromycin (minimal renal clearance — safe in CKD)
- Alternative: Ceftriaxone (biliary elimination — safe in CKD)
- Adjust dose: Levofloxacin if used (reduce to 500mg daily)
- Avoid: Aminoglycosides, high-dose NSAIDs

Scenario: CAP in diabetic patient
- Safe: Most antibiotics at standard doses
- Monitor: Blood glucose more frequently with fluoroquinolones
- Metformin: Continue unless AKI develops or IV contrast required
