CLINICAL REFERENCE: Laboratory Values and Clinical Interpretation

COMPLETE BLOOD COUNT (CBC) INTERPRETATION
==========================================

WHITE BLOOD CELL COUNT (WBC) — LOINC 26464-8
Normal adult range: 4.5 - 11.0 x10^3/uL
Pediatric range: 5.0 - 15.0 x10^3/uL

Elevated WBC (Leukocytosis):
- WBC 11.0-15.0: Mild leukocytosis — infection, stress, inflammation, corticosteroids
- WBC 15.0-20.0: Moderate leukocytosis — bacterial infection (pneumonia, UTI), severe stress
- WBC > 20.0: Severe leukocytosis — serious bacterial infection, sepsis, leukemia
- WBC > 30.0 (CRITICAL): Sepsis, leukemia, leukemoid reaction — urgent evaluation required

Low WBC (Leukopenia):
- WBC < 4.0: Viral infection, bone marrow suppression, autoimmune disease
- WBC < 2.0 (CRITICAL): Severe immunosuppression, aplastic anemia

Clinical significance in infection:
- Bacterial infections: typically WBC > 12.0 with neutrophilia
- Viral infections: typically WBC normal or low with lymphocytosis
- Sepsis: WBC > 12.0 OR < 4.0 (both extremes are SIRS criteria)

NEUTROPHIL PERCENTAGE — LOINC 770-8
Normal: 50-70% of WBC differential
- Neutrophilia (>75%): bacterial infection, stress, corticosteroids
- Neutropenia (<40%): viral infection, bone marrow suppression
- Left shift (bands >10%): early/active bacterial infection, severe stress

HEMOGLOBIN — LOINC 718-7
Normal: Male 13.5-17.5 g/dL, Female 12.0-15.5 g/dL
- Mild anemia: Male < 13.5, Female < 12.0
- Critical low: < 7.0 g/dL — transfusion consideration
- Elevated (polycythemia): dehydration, lung disease, high altitude, polycythemia vera

PLATELET COUNT — LOINC 777-3
Normal: 150,000 - 400,000/uL (150-400 x10^3/uL)
- Thrombocytopenia < 150: ITP, liver disease, DIC, medications
- Critical < 50: serious bleeding risk
- Critical < 20: spontaneous bleeding risk, platelet transfusion
- Thrombocytosis > 400: infection, inflammation, iron deficiency, reactive

METABOLIC PANEL INTERPRETATION
================================

CREATININE — LOINC 2160-0
Normal: Male 0.7-1.3 mg/dL, Female 0.5-1.1 mg/dL
- Elevated: acute kidney injury (AKI), chronic kidney disease (CKD), dehydration, medications
- Creatinine > 2.0: significant renal impairment, adjust medication dosing
- Creatinine > 4.0 (CRITICAL): severe renal failure — urgent nephrology consult
- Creatinine doubling from baseline = AKI Stage 1 (KDIGO criteria)

Clinical significance:
- Creatinine elevation in pneumonia: may indicate sepsis-induced AKI
- Important for medication dosing (fluoroquinolones, aminoglycosides)
- Metformin contraindicated if creatinine > 1.5 mg/dL (male) or > 1.4 mg/dL (female)

GLUCOSE — LOINC 2345-7
Normal fasting: 70-100 mg/dL
Normal random: 70-140 mg/dL
- Hypoglycemia < 70: diabetes medication, liver disease, sepsis
- Critical hypoglycemia < 50: immediate treatment required
- Diabetes range fasting: > 126 mg/dL
- Hyperglycemia > 180: poor diabetes control, stress response, infection
- Critical hyperglycemia > 400: diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA), hyperosmolar state

POTASSIUM — LOINC 2823-3
Normal: 3.5-5.0 mEq/L
- Hypokalemia < 3.5: diuretics, vomiting, poor intake, diarrhea
- Critical hypokalemia < 2.5: cardiac arrhythmia risk, muscle weakness
- Hyperkalemia > 5.0: renal failure, ACE inhibitors, K-sparing diuretics
- Critical hyperkalemia > 6.0: cardiac emergency, peaked T waves on EKG

ALBUMIN — LOINC 1751-7
Normal: 3.5-5.0 g/dL
- Low albumin < 3.5: malnutrition, liver disease, inflammation, protein loss
- Low albumin < 2.0: severe malnutrition, ascites, critical illness
- Significance: affects drug binding, oncotic pressure, indicates chronic illness

LIVER FUNCTION TESTS
=====================

ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) — LOINC 1742-6
Normal: 7-56 U/L (male), 7-45 U/L (female)
- Mildly elevated (1-3x ULN): fatty liver, alcohol, medications
- Moderately elevated (3-10x ULN): hepatitis, ischemic hepatitis
- Severely elevated (>10x ULN): acute hepatitis, hepatic necrosis
- Elevated ALT in setting of antibiotic use: consider drug-induced liver injury (DILI)

INFLAMMATORY MARKERS
=====================

C-REACTIVE PROTEIN (CRP) — LOINC 1988-5
Normal: < 10 mg/L (high sensitivity CRP < 3 mg/L)
- Mildly elevated 10-40 mg/L: viral infection, mild bacterial infection
- Moderately elevated 40-100 mg/L: bacterial infection, autoimmune disease
- Severely elevated > 100 mg/L: serious bacterial infection, sepsis
- CRP > 200 mg/L: strongly suggests serious bacterial infection

Significance in pneumonia:
- CRP > 100 mg/L combined with fever and WBC elevation: > 90% sensitivity for bacterial pneumonia
- Falling CRP: good response to treatment
- Rising CRP despite treatment: treatment failure, consider resistant organism or complication

CARDIAC MARKERS
===============

TROPONIN T — LOINC 6598-7
Normal: < 0.01 ng/mL (high-sensitivity troponin T varies by assay)
- Elevated troponin: myocardial injury (AMI, demand ischemia, myocarditis, PE)
- Troponin elevation in sepsis/pneumonia: demand ischemia, septic cardiomyopathy
- Serial troponins: rise-and-fall pattern suggests acute MI (ACS)
- Elevated without rise-and-fall: chronic demand ischemia, CKD, HF

LABORATORY PATTERNS AND CLINICAL SYNDROMES
============================================

BACTERIAL PNEUMONIA PATTERN:
- WBC > 12.0 x10^3/uL (often 15-20 in lobar pneumonia)
- Neutrophilia > 80%
- CRP > 50 mg/L (often > 100)
- Combined WBC + CRP elevation: sensitivity ~89% for bacterial pneumonia

SEPSIS PATTERN (qSOFA/SIRS criteria):
- WBC > 12 or < 4 x10^3/uL
- Temperature > 38°C or < 36°C
- Heart rate > 90 bpm
- Respiratory rate > 20 breaths/min
Sepsis = infection + ≥2 SIRS criteria
Severe sepsis = sepsis + organ dysfunction
Septic shock = severe sepsis + hypotension despite fluid resuscitation

ACUTE KIDNEY INJURY (AKI) PATTERN (in context of infection):
- Creatinine rise > 0.3 mg/dL within 48 hours
- Creatinine > 1.5x baseline within 7 days
- Urine output < 0.5 mL/kg/hr for 6+ hours
- In pneumonia + sepsis: AKI suggests sepsis-induced organ dysfunction

I21.9 - Acute MI (associated: troponin elevation)
E11.9 - Type 2 diabetes (associated: elevated glucose)
K74.6 - Liver cirrhosis (associated: low albumin, elevated bilirubin)
D64.9 - Anemia, unspecified (associated: low hemoglobin)
